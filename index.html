SpiritTravel App

Challenge

Design a travel app that enables users to easily access a schedule/itinerary curated from selected travel plans and activities

Goal

Users are able to receive a comprehensive schedule/itinerary displaying their travel plans and activities in one place, with the option to share their plans with others

Timeline: 3 Weeks

Research
User Persona

Grace Johnson is prioritizes a budget when traveling, takes trips on a whim, and values organizing her travel plans.

Affinity Diagram

Based on interviewees' insights, I organized 6 categories: Pain points, Preferences, Travel habits, Hobbies & lifestyle, Planning habits, and Non-negotiables.

Competitor Analysis

I learned that FlipIt was my biggest competitor but, FlipIt also offered great insight into what I could improve and incorporate into my own design.

Definition
User Insight

A free-spirited traveler with the desire to gain new unique experiences needs to have access to all the activity options a destination offers, that can be easily transferred to a simple to use schedule because, it is important that they make the most of their time traveling.

Value Proposition

SpiritTravel is a travel app that allows our users to access a simple schedule curated from an array of selected activities.

Problem Statement

I believe giving a variety of activity options that can easily be selected and inputted into a day-to-day schedule will achieve fulfillment and satisfaction of users by allowing them to make the most of their time traveling.

Iterations
  • Added a logout button

    Users were searching for a logout button but could not find one. Users wanted the ability to log out of their accounts to protect their privacy.

  • Resize and relocate the export button displayed on pictures depicting travel locations

    Users thought the original placement of the export button was not intuitive. Users wanted the export button to be larger and more visible.

  • Removed the search bar located on the home screen

    Users felt there was not a need for the search bar on the home screen. Users thought the search bar only needed to be on the screens used to find travel plans and activities or to look for previous/current travel plans.
